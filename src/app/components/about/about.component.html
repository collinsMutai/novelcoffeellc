Our Story

Our journey begins in the heart of Kenya, where we meticulously source premium coffee cherries from small-scale local farmers who pour their passion and dedication into every harvest. Direct from Farm to Cup

In our commitment to quality and transparency, we’ve established our own wet and dry mills, enabling us to process and export our coffee directly. By cutting out the middlemen, we ensure that our customers receive only the finest coffees while allowing farmers to retain a larger share of the profits. This innovative approach not only enhances your coffee-drinking experience but significantly boosts the income of the farmers, helping them invest in their families and communities.

Key factors contributing to the quality of Kenyan coffee

High altitude cultivation

Kenyan coffee is grown at elevations ranging from 1,200 to 2,100 meters or 3930 to 6800 feet above sea level, which results in slower bean development, producing a denser and more flavorful bean. This elevation means Kenyan coffee beans qualify for Strictly High Grown (SHG) / Strictly Hard Bean (SHB) status.

Volcanic soil

The volcanic soil in Kenya provides rich nutrients that contribute to the unique taste of the coffee.

Strict grading system

Kenya has a rigorous grading system, with "AA" grade being considered the highest quality, ensuring only the best beans are exported.

Careful harvesting

Kenyan coffee is usually hand-picked, allowing for selective harvesting of ripe cherries, further enhancing quality.
